Design Philosophy
Why This World Is Challenging

Real hospital operations are not run on perfectly curated datasets. They are run on a mix of polished board reports, messy spreadsheets, raw PAS exports, and tense email threads. This simulation captures the "lived-in" friction of a real NHS Ophthalmology Service Line.

⚡

Ambiguity and Conflict

Email threads contain professional disagreements between clinical and nursing leads. The agent must infer intent, gauge urgency, and navigate political dynamics without explicit instruction.

🔗

Multi-File Cross-Referencing

No task can be answered from a single file. An agent must match incident report narratives with 5,200 rows of raw appointment data, then cross-reference staffing rosters to establish causality.

🔬

Deliberate Anomalies

The data contains planted irregularities — staff sickness spikes causing capacity drops, a Medisoft-to-PAS data transfer error creating hidden risk, and budget overspend that failed to improve performance.

🏥

Domain Expertise Barrier

Tasks require genuine clinical knowledge — understanding RTT pathway clock rules, why Biometry is a cataract surgery bottleneck, or that NICE TA155 mandates pre-injection VA checks for reimbursement.

Evaluation Tasks
5 Multi-Step Agent Challenges

Each task mirrors a real operational workflow — requiring the AI agent to navigate multiple files, cross-reference data, apply domain knowledge, and produce a deliverable that a hospital manager would actually use.

01
Clinical Quality

Incident Root Cause Investigation

A patient suffered irreversible vision loss after their glaucoma follow-up was delayed by 6 months. The Clinical Director has requested a root cause analysis that goes beyond the narrative incident report to identify systemic failures.

The agent must correlate the Serious Incident report narrative with 5,200 rows of raw PAS appointment data and historical nursing rosters to prove that specific staffing gaps directly worsened the follow-up backlog on identifiable dates — then identify a systemic Medisoft-to-PAS data transfer error that masked the true scale of overdue patients.

Steps: 8 Files: SI Report v2, SI Report v1, Glaucoma Overdue List, EPR Appointments, Nursing Roster, Incident Log, Governance Minutes Deliverable: Root cause analysis report with timeline, evidence chain, and recommendations
Grading Rubric — 6 Criteria
✓ Correctly identifies 85 high-risk patients from the glaucoma overdue audit Pass / Fail
✓ Correlates 2 specific sickness absence dates (weeks 18-20) with measurable drops in clinic capacity Pass / Fail
✓ Detects the systemic Medisoft-to-PAS data transfer error that under-reported overdue follow-ups Pass / Fail
✓ Notes the contradiction between SI Report v1 and v2 timeline dates and flags it as requiring clarification Pass / Fail
✓ References the NICE NG81 guideline requirement for glaucoma monitoring intervals in the recommendations Pass / Fail
✓ Produces a structured timeline showing the causal chain from staffing gap → capacity drop → delayed follow-ups → patient harm Pass / Fail
Design Rationale Tests information retrieval across structured and unstructured data, numerical correlation, version-conflict detection, and the ability to construct a causal narrative from scattered evidence. Current AI agents struggle with the version-conflict detection — most will use v2 without checking v1.
02
Financial

Budget Variance Deep Dive

The Finance Director has flagged a £35,000 overspend in the Medical Locums budget line for Ophthalmology at Month 11. She wants a written explanation of the operational driver before the next Finance Committee meeting.

The agent must trace the overspend to its root cause (locum cover hired to clear the RTT backlog), but then critically evaluate the outcome — the RTT compliance actually dropped to 90.2% despite the spend. The task tests whether the agent will simply report the data or challenge the effectiveness of the intervention.

Steps: 6 Files: Budget Variance M11, Budget Variance M10, Agency Locum Spend, Monthly Perf Summary Apr26, RTT Snapshot Deliverable: Finance Committee briefing note (1 page) with variance explanation and recommendation
Grading Rubric — 5 Criteria
✓ Correctly identifies the £35k overspend in the Medical Locums line and traces it to backlog recovery Pass / Fail
✓ Cross-references the locum booking dates with the RTT pathway data to map spend against performance Pass / Fail
✓ Identifies that RTT compliance dropped to 90.2% despite the additional spend — does not present the intervention as successful Pass / Fail
✓ Compares M10 to M11 to show when the overspend trend began accelerating Pass / Fail
✓ Produces a recommendation that addresses the root cause (capacity vs. demand mismatch) rather than just cost reduction Pass / Fail
Design Rationale Tests critical judgement — not just data retrieval. Most AI agents will report the variance accurately but fail to critique the outcome. The "good enough" answer explains the overspend; the excellent answer questions whether the money achieved anything.
03
Workforce

Injection Clinic Staffing Crisis Response

The Matron has sent an urgent email demanding that pre-injection visual acuity checks be stopped to ease clinic pressure. She cites poor Medisoft data entry compliance (78%) as evidence that the checks are not being done properly anyway.

The agent must synthesise the audit data (78% compliance rate), recognise that stopping VA checks would violate NICE TA155 reimbursement requirements — potentially costing the trust its funding for anti-VEGF treatment — and propose a data-backed alternative that addresses the Matron's concerns without compromising patient safety or regulatory compliance.

Steps: 7 Files: Email Thread 02, Injection Safety Audit, SOP Anti-VEGF, Nursing Roster May26, Injection Turnaround Data, Staff Sickness YTD Deliverable: Email reply to Matron with data-backed alternative proposal
Grading Rubric — 6 Criteria
✓ References the 78% VA check compliance figure from the injection clinic audit Pass / Fail
✓ Explicitly rejects stopping VA checks by citing NICE TA155 reimbursement requirements Pass / Fail
✓ Identifies the root cause of poor compliance as staffing pressure, not staff negligence Pass / Fail
✓ Proposes at least one quantified alternative (e.g., reducing list size by X patients or hiring an admin runner at £Y cost) Pass / Fail
✓ Maintains appropriate professional tone — firm on safety, empathetic to the Matron's operational pressures Pass / Fail
✓ Cross-references sickness absence data to contextualise why clinic pressure has worsened recently Pass / Fail
Design Rationale Tests regulatory awareness, professional communication, and the ability to disagree with a senior stakeholder using evidence. AI agents typically either comply with the Matron's demand (failing safety) or refuse without offering an alternative (failing pragmatism).
04
Service Improvement

Cataract Pathway Redesign Modelling

A consultant ophthalmologist has proposed shifting cataract pre-assessment to community optometrists to free hospital clinic capacity. The proposal email trail went cold. The General Manager wants a data-driven impact assessment before the next service review.

The agent must extract current cataract waiting list volumes from raw CSV data, calculate capacity gains using the 72% theatre utilisation baseline, then identify a critical clinical risk buried on page 8 of the pre-assessment SOP — that community optometrists cannot perform IOLMaster Biometry, creating a data quality risk for lens power calculations.

Steps: 9 Files: Email Thread 04, EPR Appointments, Theatre Log, Theatre Util Tracker, Cataract PreAssess Pathway, Waiting List Initiative Proposal, Referral Tracker Deliverable: Impact assessment document with capacity model, risk analysis, and recommendation
Grading Rubric — 5 Criteria
✓ Correctly extracts cataract-specific waiting list volume from the raw appointment CSV (not all ophthalmology) Pass / Fail
✓ Uses the correct 72% theatre utilisation baseline from the tracker, not an assumed or hallucinated figure Pass / Fail
✓ Calculates a plausible waiting list clearance projection based on freed capacity Pass / Fail
✓ Identifies Biometry data quality as the primary clinical risk of community pre-assessment (from the SOP, page 8) Pass / Fail
✓ Acknowledges the unresolved email thread and notes that clinical buy-in has not been confirmed Pass / Fail
Design Rationale Tests numerical extraction from large CSVs, the ability to locate buried information in a long policy document, and awareness of stakeholder context. The Biometry risk is the hardest criterion — it requires reading deep into an SOP, not just the summary sections.
05
Governance

Board Governance Report Synthesis

The Quality Committee meets on Monday. The Service Line Manager needs a monthly Integrated Performance Report that synthesises clinical, financial, workforce, and safety data into a single board-ready document with RAG-rated KPIs and an executive narrative.

A pure breadth test. The agent must pull data from at least 6 different files across 5 folders, accurately match the top Red risks from the risk register, integrate the M11 financial variance, link the locum overspend narrative to the clinical backlog story, and present it all without hallucinating connections or softening the department's overall Red rating.

Steps: 12 Files: Monthly Perf Summary, RTT Snapshot, Risk Register, Budget Variance M11, Staff Sickness YTD, Glaucoma Overdue Audit, FFT Report, Governance Minutes Feb, Incident Log Deliverable: 2-page Integrated Performance Report with KPI table, RAG ratings, and executive narrative
Grading Rubric — 6 Criteria
✓ Correctly identifies and matches the top 2 Red-rated risks from the live risk register Pass / Fail
✓ Accurately integrates M11 financial variance figures (not approximated or rounded incorrectly) Pass / Fail
✓ Links the financial overspend narrative directly to the clinical backlog story — presents them as connected, not siloed Pass / Fail
✓ Includes workforce context (sickness rates, vacancies) as contributing factors, not just financial and clinical data Pass / Fail
✓ Assigns an overall department RAG status of Red and does not soften it to Amber without justification Pass / Fail
✓ Uses data from at least 6 distinct source files and attributes each data point to its source Pass / Fail
Design Rationale Tests breadth of navigation, data accuracy under synthesis pressure, and resistance to "optimism bias" — AI agents frequently soften negative assessments. The excellent answer tells the board the truth; the mediocre answer hedges.
About the Creator
Built from Lived Clinical Experience

I am an NHS Ophthalmic Technician at Bristol Eye Hospital, part of University Hospitals Bristol and Weston NHS Foundation Trust. My clinical competencies span OCT imaging, fundus photography, visual field testing, tonometry, pachymetry, and biometry — giving me direct, day-to-day experience with the patient flow, diagnostic workflows, and operational pressures that this simulation captures.

This project is not a research exercise. Every document in this workspace reflects something I have seen, used, or contributed to in a real clinical setting. The glaucoma follow-up overdue crisis, the injection clinic staffing pressure, the tension between clinical safety and operational efficiency — these are not hypothetical scenarios. They are the reality of running an ophthalmology service line in the NHS.

I built this simulation to demonstrate that evaluating healthcare AI agents requires more than generic task design. It requires the messy, high-stakes, regulation-bound context of a real hospital department — and the domain expertise to know what "realistic" actually looks like.
