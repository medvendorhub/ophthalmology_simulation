Ophthalmology Service Line Operations World

A high-fidelity simulated digital workspace designed to evaluate the reasoning, data synthesis, and operational judgement of frontier AI agents in complex healthcare environments.

Why This World Is Challenging

Real hospital operations are not run on perfectly curated JSON datasets. They are run on a mix of polished board reports, messy Excel spreadsheets, raw PAS system exports, and tense email threads. This simulation captures the "lived-in" friction of a real NHS Ophthalmology Service Line.

  • Ambiguity & Conflict: Emails contain disagreements between clinical and nursing leads. The AI must infer intent, gauge urgency, and navigate professional politics.
  • Cross-Referencing: No task can be answered from a single file. An agent must match an incident report narrative with a raw appointment CSV, and then cross-reference that with a nursing roster to prove causality.
  • Operational Reality: The data includes deliberate anomalies—staff sickness spikes causing capacity drops, data transfer errors creating "hidden" risk, and the rigid constraints of NICE guidelines (e.g., TA155 for anti-VEGF).
  • The Domain Barrier: It requires understanding niche terminology—knowing the difference between a New and Follow-up RTT pathway, or why Biometry is a bottleneck for Cataract lists.

Evaluation Tasks

Clinical Quality

Task 1: Incident Root Cause

Scenario: Investigate a Serious Incident involving vision loss due to a 6-month glaucoma follow-up delay.

Challenge: Correlate the narrative SI report with 5,200 rows of raw PAS data and historical nursing rosters to prove that staffing gaps worsened the backlog on specific dates.

View Grading Rubric

✓ Identifies 85 high-risk patients from the audit.
✓ Correlates 2 specific sickness dates with low theatre utilization.
✓ Spots the systemic Medisoft-to-PAS data transfer error.

Financial

Task 2: Budget Variance Deep Dive

Scenario: Finance has flagged a £35k overspend in Medical Locums. Explain the operational driver.

Challenge: Agents must notice that despite the high locum spend to "cover the backlog," the actual RTT performance dropped to 90.2%—requiring them to critique the data, not just report it.

View Grading Rubric

✓ Identifies £35k locum overspend.
✓ Connects spend to RTT backlog recovery efforts.
✓ Critiques the outcome by noting the RTT compliance drop.

Workforce

Task 3: Nursing Crisis Response

Scenario: The Matron is demanding an end to pre-injection VA checks due to severe clinic pressure and poor Medisoft data entry compliance.

Challenge: The agent must synthesize audit data (78% compliance), recognize that stopping VA checks violates NICE TA155 reimbursement rules, and propose a data-backed alternative.

View Grading Rubric

✓ Cites 78% audit compliance.
✓ Rejects stopping VA checks due to TA155 regulations.
✓ Calculates impact of list reduction vs. hiring an admin runner.

Service Improvement

Task 4: Pathway Redesign Modeling

Scenario: Model the impact of shifting Cataract Pre-assessment to community optometrists.

Challenge: Requires extracting waiting list volumes from raw CSVs, applying average theatre utilization rates, and identifying a "hidden" clinical risk (Biometry data) buried in an SOP.

View Grading Rubric

✓ Calculates waiting list clearance projection.
✓ Uses correct Theatre Utilization baseline (72%).
✓ Identifies Biometry data quality as the primary clinical risk.

Governance

Task 5: Board Report Synthesis

Scenario: Draft the monthly Integrated Performance Report for the Quality Committee.

Challenge: A pure "breadth" test. The agent must pull data from 6 different files across 5 folders without hallucinating connections or softening the "Red" risk status of the department.

View Grading Rubric

✓ Accurately matches top 2 Red risks.
✓ Integrates M11 financial variance.
✓ Links financial overspend directly to the clinical backlog narrative.
